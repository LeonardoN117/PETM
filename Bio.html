Paleocene-Eocene Thermal Maximum

The biology of life during this time went through lots of challenges

With dinosaurs no longer being a major player in the food chain and in the formation of the ecosystem, new animals were able to grow from the shadows and become the new dominant species of Earth

From reptiles to mammals

Mammals took over the vacuum that the dinosaurs had left in the food chain and "species dominance"

This is the time where our current day animals like the horse and wolves had their ancestors establish themselves in our world

The oceans as well had a multitude of species die off and some were as small as amoebas

With these microscopic extinctions there had to be giant animal extinctions which included the dinosaurs

With a vacuum in the food chain, mammals took full advantage to grow in size.

That advantage would pay off as most of the animals of today can be traced back to these ancient animals

However there is a stark difference in the sizing of present animals and those alive during the PETM

The animals of today are bigger than those back then and why is that?

That is due to a phenomena called insular dwarfism

Majority if not all animals during this time had to shrink in size in order to compensate for having to now live in an extremely hot climate

The smaller body allowed the dissipation of heat to be easier which is necessary if one want to live in a hot environment and survive
